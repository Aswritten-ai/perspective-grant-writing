## 2024-09-15: Reframing Grant Significance as a "Reviewer Mental Model"

We have integrated new strategic insights on grant writing, shifting our focus from research-centric arguments to reviewer-centric framing. This update establishes that a "Significance" section fails not due to weak science, but when it fails to create a "gap" in the reviewer’s existing understanding of the field. We are adopting a settled three-part structure—Gap, Consequence, and Unlock—to ensure proposals clearly articulate what becomes possible once a specific knowledge tension is resolved.

**Domains:** Grant Writing, Problem Stakes, Audience Frames
**Source:** Dr. Sarah Chen (former NSF review panel member), Workshop Notes from the Grant Writers' Collective annual conference.
